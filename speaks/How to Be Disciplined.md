## Tip Number One: Start Small - Tiny Habits

> **Steven:** Let's start with the first tip. Start small, really small.

> **Jasmine:** Wait, you mean not 2 hours a day?

> **Steven:** Exactly, that's too big. If you say "I will study English for 2 hours everyday," your brain will say "no thanks."

> **Jasmine:** True, my brain rejects that immediately.

> **Steven:** Instead, try this: "I will study English for 5 minutes a day."

> **Jasmine:** Only 5 minutes? That sounds too easy.

> **Steven:** That's the point. If it's easy, you'll actually do it.

**Tiny habit** equals a very small action that is easy to do.

> *Example:* Reading one page a day is a tiny habit.

> **Jasmine:** So the goal is not to do a lot but to be consistent.

> **Steven:** Exactly. Consistency is more important than intensity.

> **Jasmine:** That's a great sentence. Can you explain it?

> **Steven:** Sure.

- **Consistency** equals doing something regularly.
- **Intensity** equals how hard or how much you do.

So even if you study just 5 minutes everyday, it's better than studying two hours once a week.

> **Jasmine:** Wow, that actually makes me feel less stressed.

## Tip Number 2: Focus on Systems, Not Goals

> **Steven:** Now let's move to the second tip. Focus on systems, not just goals.

> **Jasmine:** Hmm, what's the difference?

> **Steven:** Great question.

- A **goal** is what you want.
- A **system** is what you do everyday.

> *Example:*
> - A goal is "I want to speak English fluently."
> - A system is "I practice speaking 10 minutes everyday."

> **Jasmine:** Ah I see, so goals are like dreams and systems are actions.

> **Steven:** Exactly. And here's the problem: most people only focus on goals but they don't build systems.

> **Jasmine:** That explains a lot.

**System** equals a set of actions you repeat regularly.

> *Example:* My study system includes listening, speaking and reviewing vocabulary everyday.

If you follow your system everyday, your goal will happen naturally.

> **Jasmine:** So instead of thinking "I want to be good at English" I should think "I will practice a little everyday."

> **Steven:** Perfect, that's exactly it.

## Tip 5: Manage Your Energy, Not Just Your Time

> **Jasmine:** Wait, what does that mean?

> **Steven:** Exactly. So instead of asking "when do I have time?" Ask "when do I have the most energy?"

> *Example:* I automate my study time by doing it at the same hour everyday.

> - Exercise at the same time
> - Follow a simple routine
